# Bench Press (Flat Barbell, Touch-and-Go) — Beginner's Guide

*Catalog: Seed #1 · Template: v2*

The most-loved and most-butchered lift in the gym. The hard part isn't pressing — it's setting up so your chest does the work and your shoulders don't take over.

## Quick cues

- Pin shoulders down and back, hold the pin all set
- Pull the bar to your chest, don't lower it
- Press up and slightly back toward the rack

---

## Step 1 — Bar Setup in the Rack

The "free unrack" rule. If you're grinding the bar off the hooks, you've already lost tightness.

- **Bar height:** with arms slightly bent at full lockout, you should be able to nudge the bar up off the hooks. Not so high you have to press it off, not so low you lose retraction reaching for it.
- **Hooks vs safeties:** set safeties just above your chest at the bottom — high enough to catch a failed rep, low enough you can still touch the chest.

> *Wrong when:* you have to fully press the bar to clear the hooks. Lower the rack one notch.

---

## Step 2 — Foundation (Lower Body and Back)

Built from the ground up. Even though it's an upper-body lift, the lower body is what makes the upper body braceable.

1. **Feet:** planted flat on the floor, slightly wider than knees, pulled back behind the knees (driving toward your butt)
2. **Glutes:** clenched and held throughout the set
3. **Upper back:** shoulder blades retracted (pulled together) and depressed (pulled down) — buried into the bench
4. **Lower back:** natural arch — small gap between lower back and bench. NOT a powerlifter mega-arch unless you train for it.

> Think: *the body is one rigid platform from feet to head, locked into the bench.*

---

## Step 3 — Shoulder Position (Working Position)

The single most important step. Loose shoulders = front delts take the lift = chest never grows AND shoulder pain.

- **Shoulder blades pinned down and back, locked into the bench**
- **Chest up** — lifted by the retraction, NOT by arching the lower back
- This protects the shoulders AND gives the chest leverage to actually press

> *Right when:* chest feels high, shoulders feel low and "buried" into the bench.
> *Wrong when:* shoulders feel rolled forward, top of the shoulder taking the load. Reset before you press.

---

## Step 4 — Grip

- **Width:** at the bottom of the rep, forearms should be vertical (perpendicular to the floor). Usually about 1.5× shoulder-width for most people.
- **Style:** full grip with thumbs wrapped around the bar. **Never use a thumbless / "suicide" grip** — the bar can slip and crush you.
- **Wrist position:** wrists stacked over forearms, NOT bent back. Bar sits on the meaty heel of the palm.
- **Squeeze hard.** Tight grip = tight everything down the chain.

---

## Step 5 — The Descent (Eccentric)

- **Tempo:** 2 seconds, controlled. Touch-and-go means *light touch* — never bounce off the chest.
- **Path:** bar comes down to the lower chest / nipple line. NOT the upper chest. NOT the throat.
- **Elbow angle:** ~45–60° from the torso, never flared to a full 90°
- **Breathing:** big breath at the top, hold the brace through descent and press

- **B:** *"Pull the bar down to your chest"* — engages the lats, controls the descent
- **I:** *"Bend the bar in half"* — fires lats and chest simultaneously
- **A:** *"Lats first, then press"* — terse, locks the rowing mechanics

---

## Step 6 — The Press (Concentric)

- **Initiation:** chest drives up first, then arms extend
- **Path:** slight diagonal — bar travels from the lower chest up to over the shoulders at lockout. NOT straight up over the face.
- **Lockout:** arms straight, bar over the shoulder joint, shoulders STILL pinned to the bench
- **Breathing:** exhale at or just after lockout

- **B:** *"Push yourself away from the bar"* — flips the mental model, locks the chest
- **I:** *"Drive the bar up and back toward the rack"*
- **A:** *"Press hard, leave the elbows behind"* — locks chest contraction at lockout

---

## Step 7 — Touch-and-Go vs Bouncing

The default is touch-and-go: bar lightly touches the chest, then immediately reverses. Touch-and-go ≠ bounce.

- **Touch-and-go:** controlled descent, light contact, immediate press back up
- **Bouncing:** bar slams into the chest, stretch reflex helps you press it. Lies about your actual strength and risks rib/sternum injury.

If you're bouncing to complete reps, the weight is too heavy. Drop it.

---

## What You Should Feel

Most bench problems trace back to one thing: shoulders rolling forward. Knowing what locked-in feels like is the whole game.

**At the bottom (bar on chest):**
- Chest stretched
- Lats engaged like you're rowing the bar in
- Shoulders still pinned, glutes still tight
- Brace held

**Driving up:**
- Chest fires first
- Triceps lock out the rep
- Shoulders STAY pinned the whole way

**At the top (lockout):**
- Chest contracted
- Triceps fully extended
- Shoulders still buried in the bench, NOT rolled forward to "reach" the lockout

**If you feel this instead → fix this:**
- **Front delts dominating** → not enough lat engagement; pull the bar down, don't lower it
- **Shoulders pinching at the top** → grip too wide OR shoulders rolling forward at lockout
- **Triceps fail before chest** → grip too narrow, OR bar path too vertical (push up and back, not straight up)
- **Lower back hurting** → arch too aggressive, bring feet closer to the bench

---

## Mid-set check

> *If something feels off, check your shoulders first — most bench problems trace back to shoulders rolling forward as you fatigue.*

---

## Common Mistakes — Fix These First

| Mistake | Severity | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bouncing the bar off the chest | Form-only / Injury-risk | Trying to use stretch reflex / weight too heavy | Touch-and-go means LIGHT touch; drop weight if you can't control the descent |
| Bar drifts toward the face at lockout | Injury-risk | Weak triceps OR pressing straight up from the bottom | Press up and slightly back toward the rack; light pause work to fix the groove |
| Elbows flared to 90° | Injury-risk | "Bodybuilder bench" myth | Tuck elbows ~45–60° from torso |
| Shoulders rolling forward at lockout | Injury-risk | Lost retraction mid-set | Re-set between every rep if needed; drop weight to learn |
| Hips coming off the bench | Form-only | Compensating for weak chest, or arched too much | Glutes stay on the bench, drop weight if you can't keep them down |
| Half reps (bar 2 inches above chest) | Strength-leak | Ego loading | Touch the chest every rep, no exceptions |

---

## Red Flags — Stop Immediately

- Sharp shoulder pain (anterior — front of shoulder)
- Wrist pain (usually grip too loose, bar rolling onto fingers)
- Chest "pop" sensation — pec tear is real, deload immediately
- Bar slipping in a thumbless grip — STOP, switch to full grip
- Light-headedness from over-bracing — exhale earlier next set

**Always use a spotter or safety pins.** Bench is the lift most likely to pin you.

---

## Beginner Programming

- **Reps:** 5 for strength, 6–10 for size
- **Sets:** 3–5
- **Frequency:** 1–2x per week — chest recovers faster than legs but shoulders need a break
- **Placement:** first big lift on chest / push day, fresh

**When to add weight:** hit the top of your rep range × all sets × two sessions in a row → add 2.5–5 lb. Upper-body progresses slow — small jumps only.

**When to back off (deload):** if form breaks before fatigue (bouncing, half reps, shoulder roll), or you fail your bottom rep two sessions in a row → drop weight 10–15% for one week, then resume.

---

## Recovery Notes

Bench is less systemically taxing than squat or deadlift, but the shoulders and pecs need recovery time. Most people can bench 2x per week if one session is heavy and one is moderate. If anterior shoulder soreness lingers more than 3 days post-session, your volume is too high — reduce sets or frequency before adding load.

## Advanced (Once the Basics Are Automatic)

### Form refinements
- *"Pull the bar apart"* — recruits lats, stabilizes the press groove
- Pause 1 second on the chest each rep — exposes weak bottom-end
- Eyes locked on a fixed point on the ceiling — anchors the press path

### Intensity techniques
- **Tempo:** 3-second eccentric, 1-second pause at chest
- **Spoto press** (pause 1" above the chest) — kills stretch reflex without changing grip or path
- **Cluster sets:** 3 reps, rest 15 sec, repeat 3–4×
- **Close-grip bench** as a separate exercise — triceps emphasis, locks out heavier
- **Floor press** — no leg drive, builds raw lockout strength

---

## Plain English

Bench press isn't an arm exercise — it's a chest exercise that uses your arms as levers. The chest can't fire if the shoulders aren't pinned. Get the shoulders right and the rest follows.

> **Plain English:** Pin your shoulders, plant your feet, lower the bar with your back, press it with your chest. Don't bounce.
