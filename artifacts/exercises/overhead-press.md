# Overhead Press (Standing Barbell) — Beginner's Guide

*Catalog: Seed #17 · Template: v2*

The hardest upper-body lift in the gym, and the most honest. No bench to push off. No leg drive on a strict press. The whole body has to hold a vertical line or the press fails.

## Quick cues

- Glutes and core locked — body is a stick
- Bar straight up; pull head back to let it pass
- Push head through the window at lockout

---

## Step 1 — Bar Setup in the Rack

- **Bar height:** chest level, just below the collarbones — high enough to dip under and stand up with the bar resting on the front delts.
- **Walk under the bar, then unrack:** bar should already be sitting on your front delts when you take the weight, NOT held floating in the air with bent arms.

> *Wrong when:* you're holding the bar with locked elbows on the way out of the rack. You'll fatigue the delts before the first rep.

---

## Step 2 — Stable Base (Lower Body)

The standing strict press is a vertical plank. If your base is loose, you'll fold forward at the bottom of every rep.

1. **Feet:** hip-width to shoulder-width apart, toes forward or slightly out
2. **Glutes:** squeezed hard — locks the pelvis
3. **Core:** braced like you're about to take a punch
4. **Knees:** locked but NOT hyperextended

This anti-buckling system is what lets the shoulders press without your body collapsing under the load.

> Think: *make yourself a stick.*

---

## Step 3 — Rack Position (Working Position)

- **Bar resting on front delts / upper chest** — not held in the air
- **Elbows slightly in front of the bar** — gives the press its initial direction
- **Wrists stacked directly over elbows**, NOT bent back

> *Right when:* the bar feels supported by the delts and rib cage, not held up by arm strength.
> *Wrong when:* wrists are collapsed back, bar feels heavy on your hands. Re-stack before you press.

---

## Step 4 — Grip

- **Width:** with the bar in the rack position, forearms should be vertical. Usually just outside shoulder-width.
- **Style:** full grip, thumbs wrapped
- **Squeeze hard.** Loose grip = bar wobbles overhead.

---

## Step 5 — The Descent (Eccentric)

- **Tempo:** 2 seconds, controlled
- **Path:** bar comes back down to the rack position on the front delts. NOT held below the chin in the air.
- **Head position:** as the bar comes down past your face, pull your head back slightly so the bar can pass — then return head to neutral once the bar is racked
- **Breathing:** big breath at lockout, hold through descent and press

- **B:** *"Catch the bar on your shoulders, don't drop it"*
- **I:** *"Pull the bar down, don't surrender to it"*
- **A:** *"Lengthen the press groove"* — terse, internal

---

## Step 6 — The Press (Concentric)

The trickiest part of OHP: the bar has to travel straight up, but your face is in the way. Solution: pull the head back to let the bar pass, then push the head forward through the "window" once the bar clears.

- **Initiation:** drive the bar straight up
- **Critical:** as the bar passes your face, push your head forward through the window
- **Lockout:** bar directly over the crown of the head, ears between the biceps, fully extended
- **Breathing:** exhale at or after lockout

- **B:** *"Push the bar up, then push your head through"*
- **I:** *"Bar over crown, ears in line with biceps"*
- **A:** *"Punch the ceiling"* — terse external

---

## Step 7 — The Overhead Lockout

Most beginners stop short of true lockout. The last 2 inches is where the shoulder fully engages.

- **Active shrug at the top** — push the bar slightly higher with the traps, lock the shoulder joint
- **Hold for a beat** — stabilize before lowering
- Without this, the lift works through reduced ROM and leaves growth on the table

---

## What You Should Feel

OHP is a full-body lift disguised as a shoulder lift. The shoulders only do the easy part — the rest of the body holds the position.

**At the bottom (bar racked):**
- Front delts loaded, ready to press
- Core braced like a vise
- Glutes squeezed, knees locked

**Driving up:**
- Shoulders pressing
- Triceps engaging through the mid-range
- Glutes + core holding the trunk perfectly upright

**At lockout:**
- Arms locked
- Shoulders shrugged into the ears (active shrug)
- Whole body still tense — no relaxation until the rep is racked

**If you feel this instead → fix this:**
- **Lower back arching hard** → core failed; brace harder, drop weight, glutes tighter
- **Pressing forward (bar drifts in front of the head)** → not pushing head through after the bar passes; cue "head through the window"
- **Shoulder pinch at lockout** → bar is in front of the shoulder plane, not over the crown; fix path
- **Triceps fail before delts** → grip too wide, OR pressing with arms instead of driving with the shoulders first

---

## Mid-set check

> *If something feels off, check your bracing — most failed presses are core failures, not shoulder failures.*

---

## Common Mistakes — Fix These First

| Mistake | Severity | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bar drifts forward (lands in front of the shoulders) | Strength-leak / Injury-risk | Not pushing the head through after the bar passes | "Head through the window" cue; record from the side to check the path |
| Excessive lower-back arch | Injury-risk | Core fails, you compensate with the spine | Brace harder, glutes tight, drop weight |
| Wrists collapsed back | Injury-risk | Bar resting on the palms instead of stacked over forearms | Stack wrists over elbows, full grip |
| Press becomes a push press (knee bend) | Form-only | Trying to use leg drive on a strict press | Glutes locked, knees fully extended; if you need leg drive, the weight is too heavy for strict press |
| Shoulder pinch at lockout | Injury-risk | Lockout in front of the shoulder plane | Bar over crown of head, active shrug |
| Half lockouts | Strength-leak | Avoiding the hardest part of the rep | Press until ears are between the biceps, every rep |

---

## Red Flags — Stop Immediately

- Sharp shoulder pain (especially impingement-style pinch at lockout)
- Numbness or tingling in arms during the press (vascular or nerve issue)
- Lower back pain — actual pain, not muscle fatigue
- "Pop" sensation in the shoulder
- Bar drifts backward and you can't catch it — STOP, lighter weight

---

## Beginner Programming

- **Reps:** 5 for strength, 6–8 for size
- **Sets:** 3–5
- **Frequency:** 1–2x per week
- **Placement:** first lift on a shoulder / push day, OR after bench on a push day

**When to add weight:** hit the top of your rep range × all sets × two sessions in a row → add 2.5 lb. **OHP progresses slowly** — small jumps only. 5-lb jumps will stall you before they help you.

**When to back off (deload):** if form breaks (forward drift, leg push, missed lockout), or you fail your bottom rep two sessions in a row → drop weight 10% for one week, then resume.

---

## Recovery Notes

OHP recovers fast (small muscle group), but the shoulder joint takes a beating if volume is too high. Most people can OHP 2x per week if one session is heavy and one is light. If anterior shoulder soreness lingers more than 2 days post-session, reduce volume before adding load.

---

## Can't Press Bodyweight Yet? Progression Path

Standing strict OHP is hard. Most beginners can't press bodyweight overhead without months of dedicated work. Don't skip stages.

1. **Seated dumbbell shoulder press** — 3 sets of 8–10 with light DBs
   → *advance when:* 10 clean reps × 3 sets with DBs ~25% bodyweight per hand
2. **Standing dumbbell shoulder press** — same, but standing (forces core engagement)
   → *advance when:* 10 clean reps × 3 sets with DBs ~30% bodyweight per hand
3. **Empty barbell strict press (45 lb bar)** — focus on path and lockout
   → *advance when:* 5 sets × 5 reps with perfect form
4. **Add weight in 2.5 lb jumps** — never 5-lb jumps until you're past 95 lb total
5. **Bodyweight overhead press** — long-term goal; can take 1–3 years for most people

## Advanced (Once the Basics Are Automatic)

### Form refinements
- *"Crush the bar"* — increases full-body tension, stabilizes the press
- *"Punch the ceiling"* — terse external lockout cue
- Pause 1 second at the rack position each rep — exposes weak bottom

### Intensity techniques
- **Tempo:** 3-second eccentric, 1-second pause at the rack
- **Cluster sets:** 2 reps, rest 20 sec, repeat 4×
- **Push press** for top-end strength (uses leg drive — log as a separate exercise)
- **Z-press** (seated on the floor, no back support, legs straight out) — kills cheating, brutal core demand
- **Behind-the-neck press** — advanced only, demands serious shoulder mobility

---

## Plain English

Overhead press is a vertical plank that happens to involve pushing a bar over your head. Your body has to be a rigid line — feet to head — for the press to even be possible. The shoulders only do the easy part.

> **Plain English:** Make your body a stick. Push the bar straight up. Push your head forward so the bar ends up directly over your ears.
