# Paused High-Bar Squat — Beginner's Guide

*Catalog: P5 #6 · Template: v2*

A high-bar squat with a 1–3 second pause at the bottom. Less weight than a normal squat. Way more leg.

## Quick cues

- Bar high on traps, big 360° brace
- Hold the count without relaxing — defeats the whole point if you do
- Lead with chest on the drive

---

## Step 1 — Bar Position (High-Bar)

- **Bar sits on top of the traps** — across the shelf made by your upper traps when you shrug
- **Not** on the back of the neck (painful, no leverage)
- **Not** low on the rear delts (that's low-bar squat — different lift, different mechanics)

---

## Step 2 — Set Up Under the Bar

- Get under the bar with feet directly under hips
- **Squeeze shoulder blades together** to create the trap shelf
- Grip the bar slightly outside shoulder width — wherever your shoulder mobility allows
- **Drive elbows down**, not back — keeps the chest up
- Stand up to unrack — don't good-morning it out

---

## Step 3 — Get Into Position

- **Walk out:** three steps max — back, foot out, foot out, set. Wasted steps drain energy.
- **Stance:** shoulder-width or slightly wider, toes turned out 15–30°, weight on mid-foot.
- **Brace:** big breath into the belly (not chest), 360° brace like someone's about to punch your stomach AND lower back, hold through the entire rep including the pause.

> *Right when:* belly is hard to the touch, lower back is tight, you can't easily talk.
> *Wrong when:* breath is shallow, belly soft, you're holding tension in your shoulders instead of your core.

---

## Step 4 — The Descent

- **Knees and hips break at the same time** (high-bar is more upright than low-bar)
- **Knees track over toes** — let them come forward, that's correct for high-bar
- **Chest stays up** — torso as upright as your mobility allows
- **Depth:** hip crease below the top of the knee at minimum
- **Tempo:** 2–3 seconds, controlled

- **B:** *"Sit between your feet, not back over your heels"* — fixes the common "sit-back" mistake that turns high-bar into low-bar
- **I:** *"Knees forward, chest up"*
- **A:** *"Screw the feet into the floor"* — external, creates hip torque and prevents knee cave

---

## Step 5 — The Pause (The Whole Point)

- **Hold the bottom for 1–3 seconds** — count it out
- **Stay tight** — don't relax into the hole
- **Stay braced** — don't exhale during the pause
- This kills the stretch reflex, so the next part is harder than a normal squat. Expect to use less weight.

---

## Step 6 — The Drive Up

- **Lead with the chest** — if hips shoot up first, you'll fold forward (good morning)
- Knees track over toes the whole way
- Exhale through the hardest part of the rep (the sticking point)

- **B:** *"Push the floor away with your feet"* — flips the mental model from "stand up" to "drive down"
- **I:** *"Drive your head into the ceiling"*
- **A:** *"Push the bar through the ceiling"* — external, locks the chest up and prevents forward lean

---

## What You Should Feel

**At the bottom (during the pause):**
- Quads stretched and loaded
- Hips loaded but not collapsed
- Core wrapped tight, brace held
- Upper back rigid, chest up

**Driving up:**
- Quads firing through the sticking point (just above parallel)
- Glutes assisting at the top half
- Bar staying in line over mid-foot

**At the top:**
- Standing tall, hips and knees fully extended
- Brief reset breath before the next rep

**If you feel this instead → fix this:**
- **Lower back rounding at the bottom** → ankle mobility issue, or you're going too deep for your build; try lifters or pause at parallel
- **Chest dropping during the pause** → upper back not tight enough; pull elbows down harder, brace chest up
- **Bouncing out of the bottom** → defeating the entire point; slow down and count the pause out loud
- **Knees caving in on the drive** → weak glutes or stance too narrow; cue "knees out" or widen the stance

---

## Mid-set check

> *If something feels off, check your brace through the pause — relaxing during the pause is the #1 reason people fold forward out of the hole.*

---

## Common Mistakes — Fix These First

| Mistake | Severity | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Falling forward at the bottom | Injury-risk | Brace lost during the pause, or upper back collapsed | Drop the weight, focus on staying braced through the count |
| Hips shoot up out of the hole | Strength-leak | Quads weak or chest dropped during the pause | Lead with chest on the drive, push knees forward |
| Knees cave inward on the way up | Injury-risk | Weak glutes or stance too narrow | "Knees out" cue, widen stance slightly, screw feet into the floor |
| Bouncing out of the bottom | Form-only | Defeating the point of the pause | Slow it down, count the pause out loud |
| Heels lifting off the floor | Form-only | Ankle mobility limited or stance too narrow | Try lifters, work ankle mobility, widen stance |
| Pause too short (treating it as a touch-and-go) | Form-only | Rushing | Count "one Mississippi, two Mississippi" before driving up |

---

## Red Flags — Stop Immediately

- Sharp knee pain (not muscle burn — joint pain, especially behind the kneecap)
- Lower back pain that lingers
- "Pop" sensation in the knee, hip, or back
- Numbness or tingling down the legs
- Sharp pain in the front of the hip or groin

---

## Beginner Programming

- **Reps:** 3–6 with a 2–3 second pause
- **Sets:** 3–5
- **Load:** 70–80% of your normal squat — pause kills your bounce, expect less weight
- **Frequency:** 1x per week is plenty
- **Placement:** main lift on a squat day, or accessory after regular squats
- **Pause length:** beginners start at 1 second, build to 3 seconds over a training block
- **Log:** pause length used (1s / 2s / 3s) — load is meaningless without it

**When to add weight:** hit all sets at the top of the rep range with a full pause and zero forward lean → add 5–10 lb.

**When to back off (deload):** chest drops during the pause, or you start cutting the pause short to grind out reps → drop weight 10–15% for one week.

---

## Recovery Notes

Paused squats are a squat variant — same CNS demand as a regular heavy squat, sometimes higher because of the time-under-tension at the bottom. Most people need **3–5 days** between heavy paused-squat sessions. If your legs are still toast 4 days later, your last session was too much.

Pair paused work with one regular squat session per week, not two heavy paused sessions.

---

## Advanced (Once the Basics Are Automatic)

### Form refinements
- *"Drive your head into the ceiling"* — locks chest position and prevents forward lean
- *"Screw your feet into the floor"* — creates hip torque, stops knee cave
- *"Pull yourself down into the hole"* — uses hip flexors to control the descent, builds tightness
- Eyes fixed on a point straight ahead — prevents head/chin drift

### Intensity techniques
- **Longer pauses:** 4–5 second pauses for advanced strength work
- **Multiple pauses per rep:** pause at parallel on the way down, pause at the bottom, pause at parallel on the way up
- **1.5 reps:** down → halfway up → back down → all the way up (one rep)
- **Tempo:** 4-second descent + 3-second pause + normal drive — brutal hypertrophy stimulus

---

## Plain English

> **Plain English:** A normal squat lets you bounce out of the bottom — like a stretched rubber band releasing. Pausing kills the bounce, so your muscles have to do all the work from a dead stop. Less weight on the bar, way more work for your legs.
